About the Book

Discover how good design makes the world more understandable and usable.

The Design of Everyday Things is a book by cognitive scientist and usability engineer Don Norman about how design serves as the communication between object and user, and how to optimize that conduit of communication in order to make the experience of using the object pleasurable.

In this book, Norman discusses the psychology behind good design and provides principles and guidelines that can be applied to create better-designed products and systems.

Author - Don Norman

Don Norman is a renowned figure in the fields of cognitive science and design, celebrated for his pioneering insights into the intricate relationship between humans and the products they encounter in their daily lives. With a distinguished academic background, including a Ph.D. in psychology from the University of Pennsylvania, Norman has dedicated his career to unraveling the mysteries of human behavior and cognition. His influential book, "The Design of Everyday Things," has become a cornerstone in the study of user-centered design.

Beyond academia, Norman's influence extends into the heart of the technology industry, where he has played pivotal roles in shaping the user experiences of iconic products. As a co-founder of the Nielsen Norman Group and a former Vice President at Apple, he continues to challenge conventional thinking, advocating for designs that resonate deeply with users.
